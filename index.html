A Tribute to
Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam
Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India" and the "People's President," was a renowned Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he was the son of a boat owner and a homemaker. Despite modest beginnings, Kalam's academic and professional journey is an inspiring story of determination and excellence.
Early Life and Education
  • 15 October 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955: Moved to Madras (now Chennai) to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: Graduated with a degree in aerospace engineering.
Career as a Scientist
  • 1960: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1980: Successfully launched the Rohini satellite into orbit using the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1983: Initiated the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • 1992-1999: Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO. Played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
Contributions and Innovations
  • 1970s: Directed the development of Project Devil and Project Valiant, which focused on ballistic missile technology.
  • 1998: Co-developed the "Kalam-Raju Stent," a low-cost coronary stent for heart patients.
  • 2012: Designed the "Kalam-Raju Tablet," a rugged tablet for healthcare in rural areas.
Presidency and Later Life
  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India.
  • 2007: Completed his term as President and returned to civilian life focusing on education, writing, and public service.
  • Became a visiting professor at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad, and Indian Institute of Management Indore.
  • Served as an honorary fellow at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore.
  • Was the Chancellor of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology, Thiruvananthapuram.
  • Held a position as a professor of Aerospace Engineering at Anna University.
  • Taught information technology at the International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad, and technology at Banaras Hindu University and Anna University.
  • 27 July 2015: Died from a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
Major Awards
  • Padma Bhushan (1981): Awarded for his work with ISRO and DRDO.
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990): Honored for his contributions as a scientific advisor.
  • Bharat Ratna (1997): India's highest civilian award for his contributions to scientific research and defense technology.
  • Hoover Medal (2008): Recognized for his significant contributions in aerospace technology.
  • Von Braun Award (2013): Awarded by the National Space Society for excellence in managing and leading space-related projects.
